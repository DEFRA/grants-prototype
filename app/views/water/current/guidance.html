Who can apply

You can apply if:

  • you’re in England
  • you grow crops for the food industry
  • you’re a nursery growing flowers
  • you’re a forestry nursery
  • you own the land the project will be on or have a lease for at least 5 years
  • the estimated project costs are over £87,500

What the grant is for

The Farming Transformation Fund water management grant is to make more efficient use of water for irrigation and to secure water supplies for crops.

The grant can pay up to 40% of the costs for:

  • constructing water storage reservoirs
  • abstraction points, pumps and pipework to fill a reservoir
  • irrigation pumps, controls, underground water distribution systems
  • water meters
  • best practice application equipment such as boom or trickle irrigators
  • software and sensors to optimise water application

The minimum grant you can claim is £35,000 (40% of £87,500). The maximum grant is £1 million.

When to apply

Grant stage Opens Closes
Check if you’re eligible 1 July 2021 31 August 2021
Full application 1 September 2021 30 November 2021
Project completed and claims made 21 March 2022

Any planning permission or abstraction licences must be in place to submit a full application.

You will invalidate your application if you start the project or commit to any costs (such as placing orders) before you receive a funding agreement.

Before you start the project, you can:

  • get quotes from suppliers
  • apply for planning permission or an abstraction licence (these can take a long time)

How to apply for a grant

Check if your project can apply for a grant

You need to check if you’re eligible and how well your project fits the funding priorities.

You’ll need:

  • information about your business (for example number of employees, turnover)
  • information about the project (for example irrigated land in hectares)
  • a list of the items you’d like to buy for the project
  • an estimate of the total cost of the items

After you have gone through the checker, you’ll get a score showing how well your project fits the funding priorities.

RPA wants to fund projects that:

  • improve productivity
  • improve the environment
  • improve water sustainability

When you are happy with your score, you can submit your details to the RPA.

You can go back through the checker as many times as you like but you can only submit your details once.

Submit a full application

The RPA will be in touch once the application period opens.

They will tell you if your project scored well enough to get the full application form.

If you submit a full application and it is successful, you’ll be sent a funding agreement and can begin work on the project.
